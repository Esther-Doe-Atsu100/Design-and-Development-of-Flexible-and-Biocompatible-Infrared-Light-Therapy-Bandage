# Design and Development of a Flexible and Biocompatible Infrared Light Therapy Bandage for Wound Healing

**Date**: November 2025

## Abstract 
This project presents the design and fabrication of a flexible, biocompatible infrared (IR) light therapy bandage for wound healing. The bandage integrates low-level infrared LEDs onto a cotton substrate, powered by a lithium-ion battery and regulated by a digital thermostat and MOSFET timer relay for controlled heat output. Experimental results showed a gradual rise in temperature from 28.8 °C to 42.7 °C and irradiance of 31 µW/cm²/nm—values within the optimal therapeutic range (40–43 °C). The device safely enhances blood circulation, tissue regeneration, and collagen synthesis, demonstrating potential for both clinical and home-based wound care.

## Results 
Experimental testing revealed a gradual rise in temperature from 28.8 °C to 42.7 °C within 20 minutes, yielding a mean steady-state temperature of approximately 42.7 °C. The measured irradiance of 31 µW/cm²/nm falls within the clinically accepted photobiomodulation window.

## Conclusions and Next Steps 
The infrared light therapy bandage successfully provides controlled thermal and optical energy for wound healing. It promotes circulation, tissue regeneration, and patient comfort while being portable and cost-effective.

**Next Steps**: Integrate wireless monitoring and biosensors to enable automatic feedback control and data logging for clinical and home use.
